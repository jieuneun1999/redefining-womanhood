WORK

In the past, women were mostly expected to stay at home and take care of their families. Their work focused on raising children, cooking, and cleaning, and even if they contributed economically, it was often unpaid and not formally recognized. Today, this has changed a lot. In the Philippines, there are now over 21 million economically active women working in fields like education, healthcare, business, and technology (Baguio Herald Express Online, 2024). This data supports the idea that women now have greater access to jobs and career opportunities compared to before. However, even with this progress, women still experience challenges such as gender stereotypes and the heavy responsibility of unpaid care work at home.

Under the leadership of Mayor Joy Belmonte, Quezon City has institutionalized a strong focus on women’s empowerment by implementing a cohesive strategy centered on economic independence and entrepreneurship. Specifically this vision is realized through the Academy for Women Entrepreneurs (AWE), which started in 2023 to give women the training they need to grow their businesses. In addition to this training, the "Tindahan ni Ate Joy" program helps mothers, solo parents, PWDs and survivors of abuse to start their own small stores by giving them starter kits. To further support local talent, the POP QC-Kolab program connects women who make local goods with professional designers to help them sell more products. Through the efforts of the QC Public Employment Service Office, the city government has provided more than 31,000 jobs to women that allows them to earn for their family’s needs.

However, while the Philippines is making great progress, women around the world still face many problems at work. For example, a recent 2025/2026 report by McKinsey & Company shows that men are still promoted to manager positions much faster than women. This broken rung on the career ladder means that for every 100 men promoted, only 93 women move up, leaving very few women in top leadership roles. Along with promotion gaps, a 2025 study in the Future Business Journal found that many women still struggle to balance their jobs with their responsibilities at home. In the end, these global challenges show why the programs that promote gender equality are so important as they provide the extra support women need to overcome these barriers and reach their full potential.
